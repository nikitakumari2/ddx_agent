Pancreatitis is inflammation of the pancreas. The pancreas is a long, flat gland that sits tucked behind the stomach in the upper abdomen. The pancreas produces enzymes that help digestion and hormones that help regulate the way your body processes sugar (glucose). Common Symptoms: Upper abdominal pain that radiates to your back; abdominal pain that feels worse after eating; fever; rapid pulse; nausea and vomiting.
