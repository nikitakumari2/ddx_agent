A common sleep disorder characterized by persistent problems falling and staying asleep. It can be acute (short-term) due to stress or a traumatic event, or chronic (long-term), lasting for a month or more. Common Symptoms: Difficulty falling asleep, waking up frequently during the night, waking up too early and being unable to get back to sleep, and not feeling well-rested upon waking. This leads to daytime symptoms like fatigue, low energy, irritability, and difficulty concentrating.
