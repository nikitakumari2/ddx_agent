The most prevalent type of headache, often triggered by stress, fatigue, or muscle strain in the neck and shoulders. It is generally considered less severe than a migraine and does not typically cause nausea or vomiting. Common Symptoms: A dull, aching head pain that is often described as a feeling of a "tight band" or constant pressure around the head. The pain is usually mild to moderate and affects both sides of the head. Tenderness in the scalp, neck, and shoulder muscles is also common.
