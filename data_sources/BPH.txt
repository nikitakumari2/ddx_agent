Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia an age-related enlargement of the prostate gland in men. As the prostate enlarges, it can squeeze or partially block the urethra, which affects urination. BPH is not cancerous. Common Symptoms: A frequent or urgent need to urinate, increased frequency of urination at night (nocturia), difficulty starting urination, a weak urine stream or a stream that stops and starts, and the inability to completely empty the bladder.
