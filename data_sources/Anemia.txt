The most common type of anemia, a condition where the blood lacks adequate healthy red blood cells to carry sufficient oxygen to the body's tissues. It is caused by a shortage of iron, which the body needs to make hemoglobin. Common Symptoms: Extreme fatigue and weakness, pale skin (pallor), chest pain, a fast heartbeat or shortness of breath, headache, dizziness, cold hands and feet, and brittle nails. A specific craving for non-food items like ice or dirt (pica) can also occur.
