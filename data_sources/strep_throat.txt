Strep throat is an infection in the throat and tonsils caused by bacteria called group A Streptococcus (group A strep). Group A strep live in the nose and throat and can easily spread to other people. It is very important to treat strep throat with antibiotics. The most common symptoms of strep throat include: a sore throat that can start very quickly, pain when swallowing, fever, red and swollen tonsils, sometimes with white patches or streaks of pus, and tiny red spots on the roof of the mouth. Other symptoms may include headache, stomach pain, nausea, or vomiting. A person with strep throat may also have a rash known as scarlet fever.
