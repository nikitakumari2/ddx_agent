Deep vein thrombosis (DVT) occurs when a blood clot (thrombus) forms in one or more of the deep veins in your body, usually in your legs. Deep vein thrombosis can cause leg pain or swelling but also can occur with no symptoms. Common Symptoms: Swelling in the affected leg; pain in your leg, often described as a cramp or soreness; red or discolored skin on the leg; a feeling of warmth in the affected leg. About half of people with DVT have no symptoms.
